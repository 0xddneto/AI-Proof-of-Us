WHY Humans use many agents; their work should not disappear.

THE ONE-MINUTE VERSION

The workday is becoming multi-agent. Human effort should be portable.

For users

Use AI normally across coding, writing, research, debugging, and local-model work. The MCP collector records private receipts.

For agents

Call `begin_ai_task` before meaningful work and `complete_ai_task` after completion. The protocol handles nonce, hashes, and replay checks.

For builders

Integrate one small lifecycle adapter that starts a receipt, ends a receipt, and exposes a `receiptId`.

PROOF FLOW

Evidence comes before settlement.

AIPOU is not raw prompt mining and not an investor pitch. It stores proof metadata and hashes, then validates receipts before any onchain claim.

  1. 01

    Wallet authorizes

    A dedicated farming wallet signs an EIP-712 authorization for one unique task nonce.

  2. 02

    Agent works

    Codex, Claude, Cursor, OpenClaw, Ollama, or another MCP client performs the task.

  3. 03

    Collector signs

    A local Ed25519 collector signs the receipt using hashes, timing, model, and usage metadata.

  4. 04

    Validator checks

    Nonces, signatures, duplicated evidence, and trust tier are checked before batching.

  5. 05

    Merkle claim

    Approved receipts enter a Merkle root. `AIPOUClaims` verifies the proof and mints rewards.

HUMAN REWARDS / AGENT PAYMENTS

Work with AI all day. Keep receipts. Claim approved rewards.

AIPOU connects the human work loop and the agent settlement loop without pretending to replace payment rails.

FOR HUMANS

Reward the workday

People who spend hours working with AI agents can collect privacy-preserving receipts and may claim AIPOU for validator-approved work.

FOR AGENTS

Receipt-backed settlement

Agents, marketplaces, and services can accept AIPOU as payment or settlement when both sides agree to use it.

FOR BUILDERS

External work evidence

Payment rails, traces, and policy tools can reference an AIPOU `receiptId` without validating AIPOU rewards or claims.

LOCAL MODE

Claims are optional

Local AI users can keep private receipts without claiming rewards or using AIPOU as payment.

EVIDENCE BOUNDARIES

Clear proof beats vague trust.

AIPOU is useful because it says what the receipt proves and what it does not prove.

PROVES

Authorized receipt integrity

A wallet authorized a unique task nonce, a local collector signed the receipt, replay checks passed, and an approved Merkle proof can mint the claim.

DOES NOT PROVE

Hidden use or objective value

AIPOU does not detect AI work that never used the MCP, trustlessly prove task quality, imply provider endorsement, or replace scanners and policy gates.

VALIDATOR

Trust tier is derived

Users cannot self-report `provider_signed`. The validator recomputes the tier from wallet, collector, and configured provider signatures.

WHAT A RECEIPT FEELS LIKE

Small enough for agents, strict enough for validators.

The public chain only needs claim proofs. Private task content stays local unless the user chooses to share it.
